Guiding Principles

Climate Change Action for all

Every person in communities has a responsibility to undertake climate smart actions that supports sustainable livelihoods for the posterity of our communities.

Right to a clean and healthy environment

Every person has a right to a clean and healthy environment and a duty to safeguard and enhance the environment.

Right to sustainable development

The right to development will be respected taking into account economic, social and environmental needs. GTI seeks to empower communities to implement a people-centered development that builds human capabilities, improves peopleâ€™s well-being and enhances quality of life.

Partnership

Building partnerships, collaboration and synergies among various stakeholders from the public, government, non-governmental organizations, civil society and private sector, as well as vulnerable communities and populations including women and youth, is a priority to achieve effective implementation of greening climate actions.

Social impact

Ensuring that all solutions initiated have a social impact component to that not only benefits the individuals but also address disproportionate vulnerabilities, responsibilities, capabilities, disparities, and equity at community level.

Special needs and circumstances

the special needs and circumstances of people and geographic areas that are vulnerable to the adverse effects of climate change will be prioritized.

Integrity and transparency

The mobilization and utilization of financial resources shall be undertaken with integrity and transparency in order to achieve optimal results in climate change responses.

Cost effectiveness

The selection of climate change interventions takes into account available alternatives in order to identify appropriate choices that provide most benefit to communities at least cost.
