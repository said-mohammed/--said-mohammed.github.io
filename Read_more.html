Kenya has over 7000 taxa and over 1200 genus yet Kenyanâ€™s plant only three to four types of trees which are exotic and have negative effect to infrastructure, people and environment. For example; Ficus benjamina and Eucalyptus species grow with buttress roots which in turn block drainage systems, crack wall of buildings and deny undergrowth vegetation (grass) in homes. Grevillea robusta and Tipuana tip which are popular in our cities and towns also attract termite under infrastructure thus creating vacuums under buildings and compromising safety and investment.

Kenya has basically created a green desert in the last fifty years by growing trees that were not ecologically positioned for her futures health and wealth and needs mitigation by reversing the trend. She will need to save and restore the environment through The National Trees of Kenya as a way of building a National culture of tree planting and providing the citizen with a list of important and useful tree species, sources, character and usage.

The main action plan in the campaign is to focus on Health, Nutrition, Energy Conversion, Habitat Safety, Immobilization, (dust absorption) phytostabilization (Absorption of metals in soils) and infiltration of rain water.
