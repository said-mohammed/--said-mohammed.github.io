The Green Teams Initiative has come of age to turn around the traditional approaches of addressing climate change at community level. The Greening Climate Actions are solutions by the Green Teams Initiative that are designed to transform the living conditions of community members into environmentally friendly systems and promote sustainable livelihoods through social impact and at the same time addressing the climate change challenges.



The Greening Climate Actions Program will have the immediate term 5 year focus on the Lake Region Economic Bloc and a select ASAL Counties in Kenya as a pilot phase; a 5 year mid-term focus replication entry into the rest of the counties in Kenya and a long term scale up phase focusing on the East African Community. The final impact phase will focus on the rest of Africa in a 20-year Green Teams vision where the lessons learnt will be shared across Africa.

The Greening Climate Action is a contributor to the attainment of the sustainable development goals and agenda 2063 of the African Union in achieving inclusive and sustainable socio-economic development in Africa.

This Program addresses the gap that exists between the complexities and technicalities surrounding climate change information dissemination, civic awareness, and capacity support for community members to deal with climate shocks and investing in real life small scale practical actions addressing the livelihoods of community members by building resilient villages.

Focus Areas

Institutional Development and Capacity Strengthening; This component focuses on building a strong institutional capacity for Green Teams Initiative to provide valuable technical assistance to the Governments, communities and individuals to scale climate actions as a national priority.

Learning and documentation; Document on film, publications and podcasts, real life stories, vivid images and experiences of the effects of climate change in communities for awareness creation and as a trigger for community led actions. This process also focuses on collecting and consolidating indigenous knowledge in addressing the climate crisis from community knowledge keepers and opinion shapers. The knowledge will be stored at community knowledge hubs that the Green Teams will establish in collaboration with key community institutions for posterity.

Civic Awareness through participatory planning processes; Mobilizing community members through community gatherings to understand climate change effects in their communities through storytelling relatable to them. During the civic awareness meetings the Green Teams conducts participatory climate risk assessments informed by consolidated knowledge to build climate scenarios for adaptation, mitigation and resilience actions at community level

Capacity Development Conduct community work lab sessions to train our people on the solutions identified in partnership with tertiary institutions at community level, local and international training and research institutions. This involves skilling/reskilling support to community members to undertake Climate adaptation, mitigation and resilience actions. A call to action through key practical actions they can take at an individual and communal level.

Connecting evidence-based research into practical solutions at community level is a key whole mark of this program. There exist myriads of studies and science that attempt practical solutions of the challenges communities face directly connected to climate change.

There is need for Innovation linked with Practical action to Greening Climate Actions

Utilization of indigenous knowledge coupled with scientific research could prove to be cost effective methods available to farmers on dealing with changing climatic conditions and provision of productive resilient seeds.

Agricultural Innovation in increasing farm yields with more resilient crops in rural farms.

Other innovations show potential to mitigate emissions by value addition to livestock feed to improve productivity and significantly reduce methane emissions.

Digital agriculture can help bring timely and customized information to farmers, improving the functioning of agricultural markets, boosting the effectiveness of extension services and making farmers more resilient to pests and weather fluctuations.

Better weather forecasts could provide customized data to farmers, help them cope with increased weather variability and provide substantial financial benefits.

Designing and providing water storage facilities, storing crops until the hunger season, or helping farmers cope with flooding and other water harvesting innovations.

Social innovations such as weather and climate-responsive cash transfers and graduation programs could help increase climate resilience and address food security needs.
